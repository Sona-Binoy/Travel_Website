Sites to Visit

Banasura sagar Dam

Banasura Sagar Dam captivates visitors with its breathtaking natural surroundings, encompassing lush greenery, serene waters, and majestic mountain vistas, making it a scenic marvel in the heart of Wayanad, Kerala.

Edakkal Caves

The Edakkal Caves, nestled amidst the lush greenery of Wayanad in Kerala, India, are a mesmerizing archaeological site renowned for their ancient rock engravings dating back thousands of years, offering visitors a fascinating glimpse into prehistoric human civilization amidst stunning natural surroundings..

Kuruva Island

Kuruva Island, situated in the heart of Wayanad district in Kerala, India, is a pristine and verdant river island formed by the convergence of the Kabini River. With its dense tropical forests, diverse flora and fauna, and tranquil ambience, it offers visitors an idyllic retreat into nature's lap, perfect for leisurely walks, birdwatching, and serene boat rides..

Pookode Lake

Pookode Lake, nestled amid the picturesque Western Ghats in Wayanad district of Kerala, India, is a tranquil freshwater lake enveloped by lush greenery, offering visitors a serene escape amidst its pristine waters, scenic surroundings, and opportunities for boating and leisurely strolls along its verdant shores..

900 Kandi

900 Kandi is a picturesque region located in the Wayanad district of Kerala, India, renowned for its stunning landscapes, lush tea plantations, and vibrant biodiversity, providing visitors with a captivating retreat amidst the serene hills and verdant valleys of the Western Ghats..

Kurumbala Kotta Hill Station

Kurumbalakkotta is a lesser-known hill station nestled in the verdant hills of Wayanad, Kerala, offering visitors a tranquil escape amidst its pristine natural beauty, lush forests, and panoramic views, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers seeking serenity and exploration off the beaten path.

Meenmutty Waterfalls

Meenmutty Waterfalls, located in the Wayanad district of Kerala, India, is a breathtaking natural wonder cascading from a height of around 300 meters in three tiers, surrounded by lush greenery and dense forests, offering visitors a mesmerizing sight and a refreshing retreat into the heart of nature.
